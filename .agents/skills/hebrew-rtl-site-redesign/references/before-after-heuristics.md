# Before/After Heuristics

Use this file when a redesign discussion is getting fuzzy.

## Homepage

### Before

- several equal-weight areas compete in the first viewport
- helper strip repeats the hero
- right-side category rail feels mirrored rather than authored for RTL
- search exists but does not feel like a strong entry point

### After

- one dominant primary action
- one clear fallback action
- first viewport explains where to start without extra reading
- right-side structures feel intentional and help orientation

## Topic Page

### Before

- large chrome before content
- too many chips, tags, and metadata rows
- reading starts late
- page feels like a system record or modal

### After

- context is short
- title block is tight
- main reading starts quickly
- secondary surfaces sit lower or appear quieter

## RTL

### Before

- labels feel translated from LTR habits
- the left side visually dominates
- context trails are long and noisy

### After

- Hebrew labels are short and operational
- right-to-left scanning feels natural
- category and breadcrumb placement reinforce orientation

## When To Stop Polishing And Rebuild

Rebuild the section instead of patching it if:

- the first viewport still needs explanation after simplification
- the page still feels like stacked boxes after shrinking
- reading still begins too low even after chrome reduction
